Games Robby Loves: Linelight

2/26/2017

0 Comments

 
I love giving games as gifts, specifically to my fiancee and mother. They have similar taste in games, enjoying many of the games I love (Age of Empires, Smash Bros Melee, etc.), but tend to prefer more puzzle- and aesthetic-focused experience. As such, I am always on the lookout for something new that they might like. And, as it happens, one such game just happened to fall into my lap: Linelight.

I played Linelight for the first time down in Atlantic City at Gameacon 2016 -- it was made by one man, an excellent guy, who (to my knowledge) crafted the game, soundtrack, everything by himself.

So why should you play Linelight? Well, for my money, no game can match Linelight's perfect balance of tranquility and intensity. It has a gorgeous and relaxing aesthetic and a lovely soundtrack, which are coupled with gameplay that is perpetually engrossing and occasionally very challenging.
Linelight is one of those games with universal appeal: it's perfect for all ages and is simple and intuitive enough to appeal to nongamers. And for me, it was the perfect video game present to get my family this past holiday season.

﻿﻿Games Robby Loves: Age of Empires II

2/26/2017

0 Comments

 
People often ask me, "Robby, don't you play any games yourself?" For the most part, the answer to this question is a bashful "No..." But there are a few serious exceptions to this rule, and chief among them is Age of Empires II.

Those of you who were there during the golden age of the RTS back in the late '90's probably remember AoE -- an awesome game in which you commanded medieval armies from all over the world. Easy to pick up, difficult to master, this game ate up endless hours back when I was young, and I rediscovered it about a year ago.

So why play AoE? Well, besides being a classic RTS with rock solid mechanics and a shocking amount of innovation for its era, it has a really commendable aesthetic, beautiful soundtrack, and TONS of attention to detail: they even take the time to have each civilization's voice acting to be done in its own language (or at least an approximation thereto)!
Moreover, AoE has seen a massive resurgence recently, with tons of people playing online, a YouTube community to follow if you like watching professional gaming or strategy tips, and even three excellent DLC expansion packs, which introduce over a dozen new civilizations and rebalance various aspects of the game.

So if your interest has been piqued, give AoE a try: make contact me (steam name: DaiRukh) if you ever want to play!
