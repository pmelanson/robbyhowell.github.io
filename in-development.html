Games in the Works

In case it hasn't been made clear already, I love making games -- at any given time I'm probably working on at least two or three in addition to maintaining any that are currently available.

The following is a small collection of the games that I am actively working on; it is very prone to shifting around as my attention waxes and wanes, but hopefully it will serve as an interesting log of projects for those who are hoping for some new offers besides the three main LAVA Games I currently have to offer.
Please bear in mind that all titles and other details given here are subject to change.

See a game here that catches your eye? Feel free to contact me to serve as a Beta Tester! Testers will be asked to occasionally try out new games they have expressed interest in to help me figure out what is needed and iron out any kinks. Also, I am much more likely to work on a game if I have people coming to me asking to Test it.
While this is a free service, Testers will not have nearly as much freedom to schedule turns and must respect their role as assessors rather than consumers. That being said, Beta Testers are also eligible for significant temporary discounts if and when the game they are assisting with is released in full.

Under Development

Listings here are given in rough order of my priorities:
  • ​Clash​ (Two Player, NonLAVA): I have always had an interest in Miniature Wargaming, getting pretty heavily involved in games like Warhammer Fantasy Battles and Pirates of the Spanish Main back in middle and high school. Clash is a tabletop battle game with its design heavily influenced by these classic hobbies and populated by races and characters drawn solely from my own LAVA Games.
  • Homunculus (Multiplayer): What would it be like if many people dwelt within the same body? Homunculus takes this idea to its logical extremes: an easily-accessible LAVA experience that can be played by large groups and does not require the heavy commitment of other games, Homunculus is an engaging and immensely entertaining challenge in which players must work together to control a single human body, taking command of all its various muscles and senses to accomplish various tasks in unison.
  • Natural Order (Multiplayer, NonLAVA): I tend to think big with LAVA Games, but there is always room to simplify: Natural Order is a group-based card game similar to Uno or Bridge in which players use natural phenomena (such as day, night, wind, rain, etc.) to defeat or reinforce the phenomena being played by their opponents. The game is designed to be played either casually or competitively and has significant nuance to its ruleset to permit for skill and strategy.
  • Crossover (Two Player, NonLAVA): Similarly to Clash, Crossover explores another of my past gaming loves: the Collectible Card Game. As an avid fan of Magic: The Gathering, I have always wanted to contribute to the genre, and Crossover is my attempt at doing so. Similarly to Clash, Crossover's source material is drawn solely from my LAVA Games, and the game itself employs a number of highly unusual mechanics to make it stand out from the rest.
  • The Eternal Parable (Multiplayer): The most experimental game I have ever conceptualized, The Eternal Parable is based around a single question: what if everyone was the Conduit of a single LAVA experience? In this challenging and usual game that pushes the limits of LAVA, all players involved work together as Conduits, performers, and world-builders to create a completely improvised and continuously-flowing LAVA story, with the only goal being to never let the Parable's momentum die.
  • Cosmystic (Single Player): LAVA Games are all about giving freedom and choice to the player; Cosmystic goes a step further. The ultimate sandbox experience, Cosmystic allows players to build their own pocket universe from the ground up using nothing more than matter and energy.
  • A Dying Tree (Single/Multiplayer): Based in a world design in loving detail around world mythology (one of my greatest passions), A Dying Tree is a challenging, party-based RPG in which the player takes the role of a third-person narrator guiding a group of heroes towards their destinies. The game has a high difficulty curve and is able to be played with two or even three players at once.
  • Crystallomancy (Single/Multiplayer): Magic and spirituality are themes that I have often explored in other games (such as Lifetime and Sacred Path), but Crystallomancy takes it further. In this game, the player takes the role of a shaman who must use crystal-based spellwork drawn from traditional and new-age thought to heal the world around them.
  • It Takes a Village (Single Player): Many LAVA Games have an "epic" feel to them, taking place on a large scale with massive worlds and grand narratives. It Takes a Village goes the opposite direction: in this game, the player serves as the ethereal guardian ancestor of a young town of pioneers. They must then work to improve the lives of their progeny and secure their place in the world, getting to know each individual member of the community as they do so.
