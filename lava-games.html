What are LAVA Games?

LAVA (Live-Action Vocal Acting) Games are interactive fantasy experiences played solely through talking, acting, and (occasionally) physical activity.

Though they bear some similarities to established mediums such as video gaming, tabletop, and LARP, LAVA is distinct from its cousins in that it is entirely contained within the imaginations of its players. Games are facilitated by hosts called Conduits, who run the world of the game and can naturally react to anything a player throws at them. This, coupled with a Conduit's rich descriptive language and and method character-acting, allows LAVA Games to be incredibly flexible, natural, and immersive.

Current LAVA Games

There have been dozens of LAVA Games over the years, but only three are currently available to the public (though more are likely to be released in the future):
  • Enclave (Multiplayer): The most popular LAVA Game of all time, Enclave is a team-based RPG with a lot of traditional elements and a heavy emphasis on acting and storytelling. It is a truly massive game that is very easy to lose oneself in, and it has an active and thriving community that is more than happy to help new players get involved! Enclave is also the main game broadcast on our LAVA Twitch Channel.
  • ​Lifetime (Single Player): The first true LAVA Game, Lifetime appeals to those who want a more in-depth and personal experience. The premise of Lifetime is simple: the player enters a colossal fantasy world and does whatever they please, seeking to live their new Life however they wish. Lifetime has been going strong for over eight years now and is definitely the most popular single-player experience that LAVA has to offer.
  • Sacred Path (Single Player): With a small yet intensely devoted fanbase, Sacred Path is a game for those who think big. An empire-management and 4X (Explore, Expand, Exploit, Exterminate) game where they player is a god ruling over their own burgeoning civilization, Sacred Path is a much more calculating and detached experience than Enclave or Lifetime but is intensely enjoyable for those​ who it appeals to.

FAQs

What distinguishes LAVA from LARP?
Though LAVA Games are certainly similar to LARPing and similar practices, a number of key factors do distinguish the two from one another:
  1. LAVA is performed without props or costumes of any kind, and its physical components tend to be controlled and representative rather than key gameplay features.
  2. LAVA Games will never have a specific plot or script, allowing them to feel more natural, intuitive, and open.
  3. The presence of the Conduit as a centralized facilitator and arbitrator as well as the small group size typically required for most LAVA Games also separates them from their LARP-style cousins.
​In summary, LAVA is a more personal and freeflowing take on live-action gaming while LARP tends to focus more on performance and spectacle.

​How much of a time commitment is a LAVA Game?
LAVA Games are quite a time sink if you get heavily involved, but they are very easy to put down and pick back up again as your schedule permits! There have been plenty of players in the past who only participate only a few times per year, allowing LAVA to be a very flexible addition to your pastimes. 
In general, the amount of time required for a "session" of LAVA and the flexibility of this time frame depends heavily on the game being played, but can usually be average out to around two hours or so.

​What exactly does a Conduit do?
Though their exact role slightly differs from game to game, the Conduit has three major roles that are retained across all LAVA experiences:
  1. To serve as the player's senses within the world, describing what is going on around them as directed.
  2. To manage the world of the game and dictate how the player's choices and actions affect the world around them.
  3. To act as non-player characters within the world of the game, allowing for real-time interpersonal dialogue.
In this way, the Conduit's role is to bring the world to life, depicting it with their voice and populating it with interesting characters while also allowing the player to interact with it in an infinitely-versatile and natural fashion.

​Can I become a Conduit?
Yes you can! I will personally tutor those who apply to become a Conduit, even assisting new Conduits in creating their own games, though it will require a significant commitment of both time and money on the part of the applicant (scholarships are available per request). It is also vastly preferable that those who wish to learn to Conduit have played LAVA Games for a significant period (at least several months) undergoing training.
