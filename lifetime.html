Lifetime

The Lifetime World is a realm steeped in history and culture, a fully-formed fantasy world ready to be explored and enjoyed. When the game begins, the player is presented with a number of Lives that they may choose to take, an inhabitant of this massive world. Once a Life has been picked, the player has complete control over what they do, living their Life however they choose as they explore richly-detailed world around them and meet many unforgettable characters.

All life must come to an end... but should a player's in-game body pass away, they will be presented with a roster of new Lives to pick from and start the cycle all over again.

In Lifetime,  players take a body in a massive fantasy world with no goal other than to live their life to the fullest. The game is played in Turns, each of which take around 1-2 hours (sometimes more) and can end whenever the player wishes. When the game is next played, it resumes immediately where the last Turn left off.
There are no real "hard mechanics" in Lifetime, allowing the game to be a completely open and narrative experience that feels much more like a personal story than a game.

Some key features of Enclave include...
  • TWO Original Fantasy Universes: Each of which inncludes dozens of unique races (each with their own language and culture), hundreds of wondrous creatures, and an endlessly-explorable world with a broad and colorful history.
    • ​Mruuntraar: The original Lifetime World with a more-conventional High Fantasy setting and many familiar creatures and races. The world tends to have a more medieval/industrial feel to it with many more cities and a heavy focus on politics and society.​
    • Preld: The new Lifetime World with a much more whimsical and bizarre setting, including completely original creatures and races that do not draw at all from common knowledge or mythology. The world tends to have a more tribal/ancient feel to it with few cities and a largely unexplored world.
  • Timeskipping: When your Lifetime body is has an established rhythm or schedule, the player is allowed to Timeskip over whole periods at once to get to more interesting events. 
  • Spirit Knowledge: Unlike Enclave, Lifetime is not about acting: players are simply being themselves in a different body and therefore have access to all knowledge that they hold in the real world. When this is applied to the in-game world it is called "Spirit Knowledge", and its presence encourages players to share what they have learned about the world with each other. This allows Lifetime, a single-player experience, to be enjoyed in groups if people are willing to watch each others' Turns.

You will probably enjoy Lifetime if...
  • You love reading, writing, or watching movies, especially fantasy and science fiction
  • You have ecclectic interests, a love of learning, or a strong yearning for adventure
  • You are self-driven and don't like superimposed goals or restrictions
  • You would like a more narrative-driven, casual experience

You will probably NOT enjoy Lifetime if...
  • You don't get easily immersed in stories
  • You are easily bored or demotivated
  • You prefer to game with a group rather than alone
  • You would like a more challenging, mechanically-driven experience
