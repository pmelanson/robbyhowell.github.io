My name is Robby Howell
And I make games!

Now, at 22 years old, I am a recent graduate from Brandeis University, where I double-majored in Psychology and an IIM (independent Interdisciplinary Major) called Interactive Storytelling.
IIM's are student-created degrees that must be approved by the university -- I was working on mine since I arrived at Brandeis. My hope with my IIM was to expand upon LAVA and bring it into a more academic light. After all, it really is just interactive storytelling when you get right down to it!

This official major from Brandeis endorsing my work came as the climactic triumph of a fourteen-year saga: I can trace the origin of LAVA Games back to the age of 7. Frustrated that my parents wouldn't let me play the video games I saw at friends' houses, I started trying to replicate them on my own through acting and imagination. Friends started playing at the age of 10, I had my first bit "hit" at 12, and then Lifetime, the first real LAVA Game, was born at the beginning of high school.
​
As such, I can safely say that I have been designing games for most of my life: it's what I do for fun, and it's what I intend on doing for a career.

But when I'm not working on games, I like to sing, exercise, and spend time with my family. I don't actually play very many games myself (bit of a point of shame, I must admit), but I do play three with some level of competency, namely Age of Empires II, Super Smash Bros: Melee, and Magic: the Gathering.

When I do play other games, I almost always do so with friends or family, especially my wonderful fiancee.
